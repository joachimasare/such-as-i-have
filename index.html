SUCH AS I HAVE is a project set apart to focus on basic schools in rural areas in Ghana, to bring the best out of students by raising them into responsible, ambitious, and mentally and spiritually well-built individuals, and orient them towards contributing to nation building.

The project seeks to bridge the gap between schooling in rural areas and schooling in urban cities, which is often characterized by constraints in infrastructure and finances. Also, as a result of underperformance of the needed effect of education on young individuals in rural areas, it has, for a long time, been an issue for rural areas to give schooling an unbiased attention.

SUCH AS I HAVE is geared towards investing into students in these areas through donations, mentorship, and spiritual impact, with the aim of building their mentality and inculcating in them the spirit of excellence and success, to inspire them to dream big, thereby giving them reason to do well in life, as opposed to settling for mediocrity.
The project, thus, seeks to raise young children of rural areas, and their parents from doubters in a bright future, into believers, by bringing countless opportunities to their doorstep.

The project aims at capturing young talents, brilliant and innovative minds, who are hiding behind being less privileged, and sponsoring them to achieving heights in society. These ones, having set admirable standards, would then be great examples to all other students from their communities that greatness is very much achievable, thereby generating more believers in those communities.

What We Do?

We develop brighter futures for many children with you

Donations

The aim of donations to both students and schools is to send across the message, “We Believe in Your Future.” As such, the aim of donations is to encourage these students to stay dedicated to their education and believe in themselves. Donations include: • School Uniforms, • Text Books, Exercise Books, Pens, and Pencils • Bibles

Koinon Events

These events are specifically held to bring together students from schools that are engaged in the project, to fellowship, and network. These events could include zonal games and quizzes which would be held to raise funds to further support the project.

Seminars

Held periodically in schools or specified locations, inviting resource personnel who have been successful in their fields, to encourage students and provide guidance and mentorship on how these students could also do same

Summer Schools

This is to teach students skills outside their classrooms.

Alma Mater Tours

To bring back the alumni of the schools in these rural areas to address, coach, and mentor current students of the schoolsr

Family Counseling

Family Counseling and Guidance for the children and their parents to support their successful education and career goals
